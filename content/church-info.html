Living Faith Christian Center

A Place Where Faith Comes Alive

Our Denomination

We are a vibrant, Spirit-filled church affiliated with the Pentecostal Fellowship of Nigeria (PFN). Our worship style beautifully blends traditional Nigerian Christian heritage with contemporary expressions of faith, creating an atmosphere where every generation can encounter God's presence.

As a member of the Redeemed Christian Church of God (RCCG) family, we uphold biblical principles while embracing the cultural richness of Nigerian Christianity. Our services feature powerful praise and worship, anointed preaching, and genuine fellowship that reflects the warmth of our community.

Our History

Living Faith Christian Center was birthed from a divine vision in 1985 when Pastor John Adeyemi and fifteen faithful believers gathered in a small living room in Surulere, Lagos. What began as a humble prayer meeting has grown into a thriving congregation of over 2,000 members, touching lives across Lagos State and beyond.

The Early Years (1985-1990)

In those early days, we met under a mango tree, with nothing but faith and a burning desire to see God's kingdom established in our community. Despite facing numerous challenges, including lack of a permanent meeting place and limited resources, the congregation remained steadfast. God's faithfulness was evident as we witnessed miraculous healings, financial breakthroughs, and transformed lives.

Growth and Expansion (1990-2000)

By 1990, through the generous contributions of members and divine provision, we acquired our first church building on Church Street. This 200-capacity auditorium became a beacon of hope in the community. We launched our first outreach programs, including a feeding initiative for street children and a scholarship program for underprivileged students. Our membership grew from 15 to over 500 believers.

Multiplication and Impact (2000-2010)

The new millennium brought unprecedented growth. We expanded to multiple services to accommodate the growing congregation and established our first branch church in Ikeja. Our youth ministry flourished, producing young leaders who are now serving in various capacities across Nigeria. We also launched our media ministry, broadcasting services on local radio stations and reaching thousands who couldn't attend physically.

Modern Era (2010-Present)

In 2015, we celebrated our 30th anniversary by dedicating our new 2,500-capacity worship center, complete with modern facilities including a children's church, counseling center, and media studio. The COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 accelerated our digital transformation, leading to the establishment of our robust online ministry platform that now reaches believers across Nigeria and in the diaspora.

Today, Living Faith Christian Center stands as a testament to God's faithfulness. We have planted five branch churches across Lagos State, established a Bible training institute, and continue to impact our community through various social intervention programs. Our journey from fifteen believers under a mango tree to a multi-campus church is a story of divine grace, faithful stewardship, and unwavering commitment to the Great Commission.

Our Mission

To spread the Gospel of Jesus Christ throughout Nigeria and beyond, nurturing believers in their faith journey, and serving our community with the transforming love of God. We are committed to creating a welcoming environment where everyone—regardless of background, tribe, or social status—can experience God's presence, discover their purpose, and grow spiritually.

Our Five Pillars

  1. Worship: Creating authentic encounters with God through Spirit-filled worship that honors our Nigerian heritage while embracing contemporary expressions of praise
  2. Discipleship: Equipping believers with biblical knowledge and practical tools for spiritual growth through systematic teaching, mentorship, and leadership development programs
  3. Fellowship: Building genuine community where members support one another, celebrate together, and bear each other's burdens in the spirit of Ubuntu and Christian love
  4. Evangelism: Reaching the lost with the Gospel through strategic outreach programs, crusades, and personal evangelism, believing that every person deserves to hear about Jesus
  5. Service: Demonstrating Christ's love through practical service to our community, including education support, healthcare initiatives, and poverty alleviation programs

Our Vision

To be a beacon of hope and transformation in Nigeria, raising disciples who impact their communities and nations for Christ. We envision a church that reflects the diversity and unity of God's kingdom, where every tribe, tongue, and social class comes together in worship and service.

Our 2030 Vision Goals

  • Establish 20 branch churches across Lagos State and neighboring states
  • Train and deploy 500 ministers and church workers through our Bible Institute
  • Reach 100,000 people annually through our media and online platforms
  • Provide scholarships to 1,000 underprivileged students from primary to university level
  • Establish a community health center providing free medical services to the needy
  • Launch a microfinance initiative supporting 500 small businesses in our community
  • Build a 5,000-capacity worship center to accommodate our growing congregation
  • Send 50 missionaries to unreached areas in Northern Nigeria and neighboring countries

Our Core Values

These values guide everything we do as a church community, shaping our culture and defining our character:

Biblical Authority
We believe the Bible is the inspired, infallible Word of God and the final authority for faith and practice. All our teachings, decisions, and practices are rooted in Scripture.
Prayer and Dependence on God
We recognize that apart from God we can do nothing. Prayer is not just a program but the foundation of everything we do, acknowledging our complete dependence on the Holy Spirit.
Excellence and Integrity
We strive for excellence in all we do, believing that God deserves our best. We operate with transparency, honesty, and integrity in all our dealings, maintaining the highest ethical standards.
Unity in Diversity
We celebrate the rich diversity of Nigeria's tribes, languages, and cultures while maintaining unity in Christ. We believe the church should be a model of national unity and reconciliation.
Generosity and Compassion
We practice sacrificial giving and compassionate service, believing that we are blessed to be a blessing. We actively care for the poor, widows, orphans, and marginalized in our society.
Family and Community
We value strong families as the foundation of society and church. We support marriages, nurture children, honor elders, and create a community where everyone belongs and is valued.
Innovation and Relevance
While holding fast to timeless biblical truths, we embrace innovation in methods and technology to remain relevant to contemporary society and effectively reach the next generation.

Our Leadership

Senior Pastor

Pastor John Adeyemi

Pastor John Adeyemi is the founding and senior pastor of Living Faith Christian Center. Born in Ibadan, Oyo State, he received his call to ministry in 1982 while studying Mechanical Engineering at the University of Lagos. After graduating, he pursued theological training at the Redeemed Christian Bible College and was ordained in 1985.

With over 38 years of ministry experience, Pastor John is known for his powerful expository preaching, prophetic insight, and pastoral heart. He has authored several books including "Faith That Moves Mountains" and "Building Strong Families in Nigeria." He serves on the executive council of the Pentecostal Fellowship of Nigeria (PFN) Lagos chapter and is a sought-after speaker at conferences across West Africa.

Pastor John is married to Pastor (Mrs.) Grace Adeyemi, and together they have four children, all actively serving in ministry. His hobbies include reading, mentoring young ministers, and playing lawn tennis.

Co-Pastor

Pastor (Mrs.) Grace Adeyemi

Pastor Grace Adeyemi serves as co-pastor alongside her husband, bringing a unique blend of compassion, wisdom, and administrative excellence to the ministry. A graduate of Education from the University of Ibadan, she taught in secondary schools for five years before fully joining her husband in ministry.

She oversees the Women's Ministry, Marriage and Family Department, and the church's social intervention programs. Her passion for empowering women and strengthening families has transformed countless lives. She is the author of "The Virtuous Woman in Modern Nigeria" and "Raising Godly Children in a Digital Age."

Pastor Grace is a certified counselor and regularly conducts marriage seminars and women's conferences. She is also the founder of "Grace Foundation," a non-profit organization supporting widows and single mothers in Lagos.
